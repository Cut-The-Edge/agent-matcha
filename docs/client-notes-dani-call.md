## 1. Members vs Clients — The Hierarchy

- Members get **first access** to matches — they say yes/no BEFORE the other party is even contacted.
- Members get **6 matches per quarter** (2 per month).
- **Membership pricing:**

  - $200 to speak to Dani (founder, premium)
  - Free tier = events access only, added to database

## 2. Match Flow — HOW IT ACTUALLY WORKS (Critical!)

**Matches are NOT auto-generated by SmartMatchApp's algorithm.** Dani MANUALLY creates intros.

### The real flow:
1. Dani picks a match pair manually
2. She moves both people to **"Active Introductions"** group in SmartMatchApp
3. She clicks **"Create Intro"** using a saved **intro preset** template
4. The intro is sent via **email** (SMS possible but never tested, WhatsApp TBD)
5. Member receives email with match details and clicks to view

### What the intro preset includes:
- Photo
- First name
- Location
- Birthday
- Occupation
- Faith
- Political affiliation (if available)
- Children info (if available)
- **Matchmaker Notes** = the AI summary from phone intake call

> **Important:** Not everyone has all fields. If a field isn't filled out, it simply doesn't appear in the intro.

### Match categories in SmartMatchApp:
| Category | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **Active Introductions** | Currently being worked on |
| **Rejected Introductions** | Member said NO (with feedback) |
| **Past Introductions** | Didn't reject but didn't proceed — holding zone, can revisit later |

> **Architecture Impact:** CUT-249 (Match Event Listener) needs to be rethought. The trigger is NOT SmartMatchApp auto-generating matches. It's Dani manually creating an intro. We need to detect when she moves someone to "Active Introductions" or creates an intro.

## 3. Match Trigger Mechanism — RESOLVED

**The trigger for the WhatsApp bot is: Dani creates an intro in SmartMatchApp.**

Options to detect this:
- SmartMatchApp webhook on "intro created" or "status changed to Active Introductions"
- SmartMatchApp Zapier trigger: "New, Updated or Deleted Client" (when moved to Active Introductions)
- Poll the Active Introductions group for new entries
- Manual trigger from our dashboard (Dani clicks "Start match flow" after creating the intro)

> **Recommended:** Start with manual dashboard trigger. Dani creates intro in SMA, then clicks a button in our dashboard to kick off the WhatsApp flow for that match pair. Simple, reliable, no SMA API dependency for triggering.

## 4. Voice Agent — NOT a Profile Form

**The voice agent is NOT for collecting profile fields.** This is a critical clarification.

### What the voice agent IS for:
- A **deeper conversation** — getting to know the person beyond their form answers
- Understanding personality, vibe, what they're really looking for
- Building the "matchmaker notes" that make intros personal

### What the voice agent is NOT for:
- Collecting structured profile fields (name, age, occupation, etc.)
- That's a **20-25 minute online form** that they fill out separately
- If profile fields are missing after the call, send them a **link to complete their profile**

### Call structure (from Dani's description):
1. "Hi, I'm Matcha. Thank you for calling in."
2. "This call is to get a deeper understanding of who you are and what you're looking for."
3. "If you haven't completed your profile, we'll send you a profile update request after the call."
4. Deeper questions and conversation
5. Closing: "Thank you. We're updating your profile to reflect everything you've told us."
6. Upsell: Offer membership/VIP packages
7. Post-call: Send profile completion link if needed

### Expected duration:
- With Dani: 30-40 minutes
- With Jane: 15-30 minutes
- With AI agent: **10-20 minutes** (Dani's estimate)
- People are NOT paying for the AI call, so they won't linger

### What gets saved:
- Full transcript → **Profile Notes** ("Allenby Notes")
- AI-generated summary → **Custom field on profile** (used in intro presets as "Matchmaker Notes")

## 6. Personal Outreach Upsell — $250 Split 50/50

### How it works:
- Member receives a match intro but wants **more information**
- Bot offers: "For $250, we'll personally reach out to the other person"
- **Split payment:** $125 upfront, $125 only if the other party agrees to connect
- Dani/Jane **personally calls** the other person and "pitches" the member
- This is a **HUMAN task** — not AI. Just notify Dani when payment is received.

### Flow:
1. Member selects "More Information" in WhatsApp
2. Bot explains: no more info available in profile, but offers Personal Outreach
3. Member pays $125 via Stripe link
4. System pings Dani: "[Member] paid $125 for Personal Outreach on [Match Name]"
5. Dani calls the other person manually
6. If other person agrees → charge remaining $125, proceed to introduction
7. If other person declines → no additional charge, inform member

> **Decision confirmed:** Personal Outreach stays human for now. Can be automated later if volume demands it.

## 7. "Not Interested" Feedback Flow — More Nuanced Than SOW

When a member says "Not Interested," the bot should:

1. Ask WHY with **quick-tap options**:
   - Physical attraction
   - Lifestyle mismatch
   - Location
   - Age
   - Values/beliefs
   - Something else (free text)
2. **Also allow voice notes or free text** for richer data (AB test this)
3. Thank them and confirm: "Got it. We'll refine your preferences accordingly."
4. Move match from Active → **Rejected Introductions** in SMA
5. Save all feedback to **Match Notes**

### After 3 declined matches:
- **Pause** before sending another profile
- Offer a **recalibration call**: "It's usually helpful to recalibrate together. Please book a quick alignment call."
- This call is **FREE** (they're already paying members)
- 3 rejections = halfway through their 6 matches per quarter
- Book via link (same calendar system)

> **Architecture Impact:** Need a counter per member tracking consecutive rejections. After 3, trigger recalibration flow instead of next match.

---

## 8. "Past Introductions" — The Holding Zone

When a member doesn't reject but doesn't proceed (e.g., declines the upsell but doesn't say no to the match):
- Move to **Past Introductions** (not Rejected)
- Bot says: "No problem. If something shifts or you'd like us to reach out later, just ping us with their name."
- This is a **soft no** — they can be revisited

> Different from Rejected: Rejected = definitive no with feedback. Past = undecided/holding.
